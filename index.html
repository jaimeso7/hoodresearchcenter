What is Hood Research Center?

Hood Research Center isn’t a formal institution. It’s a mindset: we stop waiting for perfect programs or fancy labs and start using the tools we have—friends, the internet, and our own grind—to answer real questions that matter to us.

Each “research problem” can be anything that helps us move forward:

  • Careers: Which skills actually get people hired from our background?
  • Money: What side hustles or paths actually scale without burning out?
  • School: What study patterns help us pass hard classes while working jobs?
  • Life: How do we balance mental health, family, and ambition?

Instead of guessing, we treat these like mini research projects: collect data, test ideas, and share what works so the whole group eats.

How we work (simple version)

We keep the process lightweight and human:

  • 1. Pick a question – Something real: not theoretical flex, but a problem we’re living.
  • 2. Gather receipts – Stories, data, surveys, job posts, schedules, whatever we can get.
  • 3. Test small changes – New routines, tools, workflows, or ways of reaching out.
  • 4. Share the playbook – Document what worked so the next person doesn’t start from zero.
Who is this for?

People from underrepresented backgrounds, first-gen students, or anyone who feels like they weren’t “supposed” to be in research, tech, or academia but showed up anyway.
